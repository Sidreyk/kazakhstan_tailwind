Traditional Kazakhstan Delicacies

Kazakh cuisine reflects the nation's nomadic heritage and rich cultural traditions. Known for its use of horse meat, mutton, dairy products, and various traditional bread types, the cuisine offers unique flavors and experiences.

Beshbarmak

The national dish of Kazakhstan, consisting of boiled horse meat or lamb with flat noodles and onion sauce.

• Traditional celebration dish

• Eaten with hands

• Served on special occasions

Kazy

Traditional horse meat sausage, a delicacy in Kazakh cuisine made from rib meat and fat.

• Premium delicacy

• Served sliced

• Rich in flavor

Baursak

Traditional fried dough, a type of bread that's puffy and golden in color.

• Essential festive food

• Served with tea

• Popular street food

Kuyrdak

A traditional dish made from roasted liver, heart, and kidneys with onions and peppers.

• Hearty dish

• Rich in nutrients

• Traditional recipe

Shubat

Fermented camel milk, a traditional beverage rich in vitamins and minerals.

• Healthy drink

• Unique taste

• Traditional beverage

Kumys

Fermented mare's milk, considered the national drink of Kazakhstan.

• National beverage

• Ceremonial drink

• Historical significance
